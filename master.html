Welcome to Boundary Layer Ceilometer Retrieval’s documentation!¶

This is the documentation for the retrieval of boundary layer structure from Ceilometer Data from the Spectroscopy and Remote Sensing (EPR) Group at UNAM, Mexico.

Since 2008, a commercial lidar system, the Vaisala CL31 ceilometer, has been measuring backscattering profiles with a \(\sim 10\) [m] vertical resolution and a temporal resolution ranging from 2 to 16 s. Through the analysis of these profiles, [GarciaFSB+18] showed the typical diurnal and seasonal variability of the mixed-layer height.

Figure 1. Ceilometer Vaisala CL31. This instrument is an optical active remote sensing instrument equipped with an eye-safe laser that interacts with atmospheric aerosols in such a way that the backscattered signal has been found to be a good proxy for aerosol distribution [Bro03][GarciaFSB+18].
